Popcorn with Nutritional Yeast

A unique topping for stovetop popcorn!

Popcorn made on the stove is an economical treat. Popcorn kernels are cheap to buy and can be stored for a long time.

This popcorn recipe uses browned butter, nutritional yeast, and hot sauce as a delicious topping.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup popcorn kernels
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil
  • 1/4 cup salted butter
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • Hot sauce to taste (Franks is my recommendation)

Steps:

  1. In a medium pot with lid, add the oil and kernels. The pot and lid should be heavy, with thick walls, else poor heat distribution will cause burnt kernels and oil.
  2. Turn heat to medium-high. Listen for popping. Once it begins popping, shake pot regularly.
  3. Once popping slows down to once every 3 seconds, take pot off heat and dump popcorn into a bowl.
  4. Return pot to stove. Add butter. Using the residual heat, melt the butter. Turn the element on to medium and brown your butter.
  5. Once butter is browned, add several dashes of hot sauce, then immediately pour over popcorn.
  6. Sprinkle nutritional yeast on popcorn and toss to distribute toppings evenly.
